# User Interviews

## Interview 1

**Name:** A.K. kishor
**Role:** Founder  
**Company Stage:** Bootstrapped SaaS startup with 4 employees

### Direct Quotes

1. "We subscribe to ChatGPT, Cursor, and GitHub Copilot, but I don't know the total monthly cost."
2. "The bigger problem is unused seats that stay active after interns leave."
3. "If a tool showed exact savings in dollars, I would use it immediately."

### Most Surprising Thing They Said

The most surprising comment was that unused seats were a larger source of waste than expensive plans. I expected pricing tiers to be the main concern, but the founder was more worried about subscriptions that remained active after team members stopped using them.

### What It Changed About My Design

This conversation convinced me to emphasize the savings numbers prominently on the results page. It also led me to add shareable URLs and PDF downloads so founders could forward audit results to co-founders and finance partners. I also made sure the recommendations focused on both pricing changes and seat optimization rather than only suggesting alternative tools.

## Interview 2

**Name:** R.S.Ramachandra  
**Role:** Engineering Manager  
**Company Stage:** Seed-stage startup with 35 employees

### Direct Quotes

1. "My developers choose tools faster than I can track the subscriptions."
2. "I care more about cost per developer than total spend."
3. "If you benchmark us against similar teams, that would be very useful."

### Most Surprising Thing They Said

The most surprising insight was that total spend was less important than spend per developer. This reframed the problem from a budgeting exercise to a productivity efficiency metric.

### What It Changed About My Design

Based on this feedback, I added Benchmark Mode. The results page now calculates AI spend per developer and compares it to an industry estimate of $35 per developer. This made the product more actionable for engineering leaders who want a quick performance benchmark instead of just raw cost numbers.

## Interview 3

**Name:** M.P.vedha nadha 
**Role:** Solo Founder  
**Company Stage:** Pre-revenue side project

### Direct Quotes

1. "I would only trust the recommendations if I could see where the pricing data comes from."
2. "A PDF report makes the tool feel much more professional."
3. "Referral codes are a clever way to encourage sharing."

### Most Surprising Thing They Said

The most surprising observation was that documentation and transparency affected trust more than the recommendations themselves. The founder wanted traceable pricing sources before acting on any advice.

### What It Changed About My Design

This interview led me to create `PRICING_DATA.md` with official vendor pricing URLs and verification dates. It also reinforced the importance of PDF export and referral codes. As a result, the product became more credible and more likely to be shared with other founders.
